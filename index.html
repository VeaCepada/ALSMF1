ALS Offers

The Alternative Learning System (ALS) offers a variety of programs designed to provide non-formal education opportunities for Filipinos, particularly those who haven't completed formal education. These programs include basic literacy development and pathways to complete elementary and secondary education. ALS also offers various learning modalities to cater to diverse needs.

Accreditation and Equivalency (A&E):

This program allows individuals to complete Elementary and Junior High School Education. The ALS A&E Assessment and Certification is a process that comprises an exam and other appropriate assessments to measure the competencies acquired by ALS learners based on the ALS K to 12 Basic Education Curriculum (BEC).

Basic Literacy Program (BLP):

This program focuses on developing essential literacy skills like reading, writing, and numeracy.

Community-Based Learning:

ALS often emphasizes community-based learning, promoting self-help and community development.

Community ALS Implementers

Individuals engaged by DepEd or LGUs to deliver ALS programs. CAI's engage with diverse learners, assess their needs, develop learning plans, and facilitate learning sessions. Key responsibilities include advocacy, community organization, and monitoring/evaluation of the program.
